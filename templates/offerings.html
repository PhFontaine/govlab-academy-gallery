Offerings from the GovLab Academy

Overview

The Academy is the GovLab’s educational and training initiative focused on bringing citizen-driven innovation to pressing public problems.

What are public problems? Often they involve inefficiencies in governance including instances when local agencies fail to provide services, spend public funds, and manage operations in accordance with citizen interests. But public problems also exist outside of government. The ease with which we can hail a cab or find out the train schedule or learn about child care options in a local community are public problems as well.

These are problems that impact our experiences as citizens and neighborhood residents . The GovLab is interested in expanding the pool of individuals, organizations, and agencies that participate in addressing these issues.

Courses

Solving Public Problems With Technology (“SPPWT”) is a semester- long course through which participants (1) become proficient in current tech and design practices implemented by civic hacking communities, and (2) develop their own projects. This course in partnership with NYU, MIT’s Media Lab, and Arizona State University, and is offered to enrolled university students for credit and to non-credit online participants. Workshops

Our experience is that even the most well-intended individuals tend to equate the task of defining a problem with naming – and urgently getting to work on – a solution. Their perfectly understandable impulse is to devote the lion’s share of energy and attention to what visibly needs to get built or changed or implemented, not to the painstaking, unfashionable, upstream effort to define – with true rigor – what the precise problem is on which they need to focus. As a result, they waste scarce time and resource and credibility on addressing symptoms, not underlying causes.

That is why the GovLab Academy now offers a short, easily customized, 2-day “problem defining” Workshop to city officials collaborating in small teams on a specific issue or set of issues. Through an iterative process of deploying group effort against the real issues faced by the real cities in which they work -- framing, critique, revision, more critique, and revision yet again -- the Workshop helps all participants master the tools, techniques, and approaches essential to the discipline of genuinely rigorous problem definition.

These Workshops have now been successfully run with teams of officials from cities in both the US and Canada. “We thought we had squeezed all the sloppiness out,” one noted, “ but we were wrong. It was a little embarrassing how wrong we were. By tightening and tightening our understanding of the problem we were tackling, we were able to shape projects that would be easier to implement and much more likely to deliver real value.”
