Empowering the next generation of civic change agents

The Academy is the GovLab’s educational and training initiative focused on bringing citizen-driven innovation to pressing public problems.

What are public problems?

Often they involve inefficiencies in governance including instances when local agencies fail to provide services, spend public funds, and manage operations in accordance with citizen interests. But public problems also exist outside of government. The ease with which we can hail a cab or find out the train schedule or learn about child care options in a local community are public problems as well. These are problems that impact our experiences as citizens and neighborhood residents.
The GovLab is interested in expanding the pool of individuals, organizations, and agencies that participate in addressing these issues.

Who do we help?

Often those who have the best ideas about improving the way that we experience cities are people who live in them and work in them. However, they might not have the skills, experience,or resources to implement an actionable project, campaign, or venture. The Academy’s goal is to provide the support needed to get these projects operating and making impact.

What we provide?

  • coaching to understand and define the problem at issue
  • strategic guidance in project development
  • mentor and peer networking opportunities
  • communication skills training
  • research training to understand the problem and hone in on the solution
  • connections to experts in the field
  • skillshares
  • courses about how to implement new technologies and design principles
  • access to funders

Courses

Solving Public Problems With Technology (“SPPWT”) is a semester-long course through which participants (1) become proficient in current tech and design practices implemented by civic hacking communities, and (2) develop their own projects. This course in partnership with NYU, MIT’s Media Lab, and Arizona State University, and is offered to enrolled university students for credit and to non-credit online participants. Workshops

The Academy works with municipal governments to train their staff in innovation and human-centered design. These courses are led by Alan Kantrow and held in person. Typically they are 1-3 days in length and are focused on learning rather than building.
